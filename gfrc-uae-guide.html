01 / What is GFRC?

Glass Fibre Reinforced Concrete — known interchangeably as GRC in the UAE and broader GCC region — is a composite material made from Portland cement, fine aggregates, alkali-resistant glass fibres, and water. The glass fibres replace conventional steel reinforcement, resulting in a material that is significantly lighter than standard concrete while retaining excellent compressive strength and surface finish quality.

In the UAE, GFRC has become the preferred material for feature façades, ornamental cladding, cornices, columns, and custom architectural elements. Its ability to be cast into virtually any shape or profile makes it uniquely suited to the high-specification, design-led projects that dominate Dubai and Abu Dhabi's construction pipeline.

UAE TERMINOLOGY NOTE

In the UAE and GCC, GFRC is most commonly referred to as GRC (Glass Reinforced Concrete). Both terms refer to the same material. When specifying or sourcing locally, use GRC to ensure suppliers understand the requirement immediately.

02 / Why GFRC Dominates UAE Façades

The UAE's built environment presents specific technical challenges that GFRC addresses better than most alternatives:

Extreme Heat Performance

Ambient temperatures in Dubai and Abu Dhabi regularly exceed 45°C in summer. GFRC panels have excellent thermal mass — they absorb heat during the day and release it slowly, reducing peak thermal load on building envelopes. The material's low thermal conductivity (approximately 0.5–1.0 W/mK) makes it well suited to GCC climate conditions.

Humidity and Salt Resistance

Coastal UAE locations — particularly Dubai Marina, Palm Jumeirah, Abu Dhabi Corniche, and Ras Al Khaimah waterfront developments — expose façade materials to high humidity and salt-laden air. GFRC's dense cement matrix, when correctly specified and manufactured, provides strong resistance to chloride ingress and carbonation, outperforming conventional concrete in these conditions.

Architectural Freedom

UAE architecture — particularly hospitality, luxury residential, and civic buildings — demands complex geometries, deep reveals, ornamental detailing, and large-format custom panels. GFRC can be cast into virtually any profile, including double-curved surfaces, deep coffered panels, and intricate mashrabiya-inspired screens, without the structural penalty of solid concrete.

CRITICAL: POST-GRENFELL REGULATIONS IN UAE

Following the 2017 Grenfell Tower fire, Dubai Civil Defence and Abu Dhabi's regulatory bodies significantly tightened façade material requirements for buildings above 15 metres. GFRC is non-combustible and unaffected by these restrictions, making it one of the few materials that can be freely specified on high-rise towers without requiring special DCD approval for the cladding system itself. Always verify that fixings, cavity insulation, and sealants within the system also meet non-combustible requirements.

07 / Common Mistakes to Avoid

Under-specifying the fixing system

The GFRC panel is only as good as its fixing system. In the UAE's high-wind and seismic zones, fixings must be engineered specifically for the project. Stainless steel Grade 316 fixings are mandatory for coastal projects. Never accept a generic fixing specification from a supplier — require project-specific engineered drawings.

Ignoring thermal movement

UAE temperature differentials between night and day, and between summer and winter, can reach 30–40°C. GFRC panels will expand and contract accordingly. Movement joints at panel perimeters must be sized to accommodate this — typically 15–20mm minimum — and filled with appropriate sealant, not mortar.

Selecting suppliers without DCD-approved test certificates

Not all GFRC manufacturers in the UAE hold current DCD-approved fire test certificates. Always request the original test certificate, verify the issuing laboratory is accredited, and confirm the certificate covers the specific mix design and thickness you are specifying. Certificates for one mix design do not automatically apply to others.
