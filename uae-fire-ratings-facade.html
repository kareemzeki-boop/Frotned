Section 01

Regulatory Framework Overview

Fire safety requirements for external facade cladding in the UAE are governed by a layered regulatory structure comprising national legislation, emirate-level civil defence authority requirements, and referenced international standards. The primary instruments are:

  • UAE Fire and Life Safety Code 2017 — the national framework standard governing fire safety in buildings. Adopted across all emirates and enforced by the relevant civil defence authority in each jurisdiction.
  • Dubai Civil Defence (DCD) — the fire safety enforcement authority in Dubai. Issues technical circulars, processes Technical Approval applications, and conducts inspections. DCD circulars supersede general Code provisions where they introduce more stringent requirements.
  • Abu Dhabi Civil Defence (ADCD) — the equivalent authority in Abu Dhabi. Requirements are broadly aligned with DCD but are administered independently; projects in Abu Dhabi must comply with ADCD, not DCD.
  • Trakhees (EHS) — the civil defence authority for JAFZA, Palm Jumeirah, and other Dubai free zone developments. Applies DCD standards with additional project-specific requirements.
  • MoIAT Law No. 3 of 2026 — the Ministry of Industry and Advanced Technology mandatory standards framework, which from 2026 requires ECAS (Emirates Conformity Assessment Scheme) certification for specified construction products, including facade cladding materials entering the UAE market.

The EN 13501-1 standard (Fire Classification of Construction Products and Building Elements) is the reference classification system adopted by the UAE Fire and Life Safety Code 2017 for external cladding fire reaction classification. All cladding materials must be classified to EN 13501-1 by an accredited laboratory and the classification certificate submitted as part of the DCD or ADCD Technical Approval package.

Section 02

EN 13501-1 Fire Classification System

EN 13501-1 classifies the fire reaction performance of construction products on the basis of small flame ignition resistance, heat release rate, flame spread, and smoke production. The classification is determined by two mandatory test methods: ISO 11925-2 (small flame exposure — ignitability of building products) and EN 13823 (single burning item, SBI — heat and smoke release). Class A1 materials are assessed by EN ISO 1182 (non-combustibility) and EN ISO 1716 (gross heat of combustion) without SBI testing.

A1
Non-combustible. No contribution to fire in any stage. Required for high-rise cladding.
A2
Negligible contribution to fire. Permitted for cladding on all UAE building heights when tested as composite system.
B
Very limited combustibility. Permitted for cladding below 23 m in UAE subject to DCD approval.
C
Limited combustibility. Permitted below 15 m for non-primary cladding elements only. Rarely accepted by DCD.
D–F
Combustible or unclassified. Not permitted for external cladding on any UAE building.

Smoke and Droplet Sub-classifications

The EN 13501-1 classification includes mandatory sub-classifications for smoke production (s1, s2, s3) and flaming droplet or particle production (d0, d1, d2). For UAE external cladding applications, DCD requires smoke production class s1 or s2 and droplet class d0 for all materials at or above Class A2. A full classification designation for a compliant external cladding material is therefore expressed as, for example, A2-s1, d0.

Sub-classificationSmoke (s)Droplets (d)
s1 / d0Low smoke production; smoke growth rate limitedNo flaming droplets or particles produced
s2 / d1Medium smoke productionFlaming droplets or particles produced for no more than 10 seconds
s3 / d2High smoke production — no limitFlaming droplets or particles produced beyond 10 seconds — not acceptable for UAE cladding
Classification Scope

EN 13501-1 classification applies to the cladding material or composite system as tested — not to individual components in isolation. Where an ACM panel comprises an aluminium skin and a mineral FR core, the classification must be obtained for the complete panel construction as manufactured and installed. Test data for the core material alone, or for the aluminium facing alone, is not a valid substitute for a composite system certificate.

Critical Requirement

DCD engineering practice since 2020 has applied an effective requirement of Class A2 or better for all primary external cladding at all building heights, regardless of the Code height thresholds. Combustible cladding materials (Class B–F) are processed on a case-by-case basis requiring a full fire engineering assessment and DCD Technical Approval. Specifiers should design to A2 minimum as the baseline unless the project fire engineer advises otherwise based on a completed DCD pre-consultation.

Section 04

DCD Circular 2/2020 — ACM and Combustible Cladding

Following the 2017 Grenfell Tower fire in London and a series of high-rise fires in the UAE involving combustible aluminium composite cladding with polyethylene (PE) cores, Dubai Civil Defence issued Circular 2/2020 establishing specific prohibition and control measures for combustible cladding materials on existing and new UAE buildings.

Key provisions of DCD Circular 2/2020:

  • PE-core ACM prohibition: Aluminium Composite Material with polyethylene or low-density polyolefin cores is prohibited for external cladding on all new construction in Dubai, regardless of building height. Existing buildings with PE-core ACM are subject to mandatory remediation programmes.
  • FR mineral core ACM: ACM panels with a verified fire-retardant mineral core containing a minimum 70% inorganic content by weight are permitted where the composite panel system achieves EN 13501-1 Class A2-s1, d0 or better by independent test. Core composition must be verifiable from the material safety data sheet and third-party certification.
  • Combustible cladding on existing buildings: Owners of buildings above 18 metres with combustible facade cladding are required to engage a DCD-approved fire engineer to assess remediation requirements. The circular established a phased remediation timeline.
  • Technical Approval requirement: All new cladding material specifications must be submitted to DCD for Technical Approval before installation commences. Submissions include EN 13501-1 test certificates, material data sheets, fire engineering assessment where applicable, and a declaration of conformity from the installer.
ACM Verification Requirement

The prohibition of PE-core ACM has generated a market of products claiming FR-core compliance without adequate independent verification. Specifiers must require the following for any ACM product: (1) EN 13501-1 composite panel test certificate from a UKAS or DAkkS-accredited laboratory; (2) core material data sheet confirming inorganic content by weight with third-party verification; (3) DCD-issued or DCD-accepted product acceptance letter specific to the product reference, not a generic material type. Generic certificates covering a class of products are not acceptable for DCD submission.

Section 06

Testing and Certification Requirements

DCD requires that cladding material fire classification certificates originate from accredited laboratories. The following accreditation bodies are accepted for UAE regulatory purposes:

  • UKAS (United Kingdom Accreditation Service) — UK national accreditation body
  • DAkkS (Deutsche Akkreditierungsstelle) — German national accreditation body
  • COFRAC (Comite Francais d’Accreditation) — French national accreditation body
  • Emirates National Accreditation System (ENAS) — UAE national accreditation body; growing acceptance for UAE-based laboratory testing

Test certificates must specify the exact product reference, dimensions, configuration (including core and facing specifications for composite products), the EN 13501-1 classification achieved, the date of test, and the accreditation body under which the testing laboratory operated. Expired or out-of-scope certificates are not accepted; DCD typically requires certificates issued within 5 years of the Technical Approval submission date.

ECAS Certification (MoIAT Law No. 3 of 2026)

From 2026, facade cladding products imported into or manufactured in the UAE and covered by Emirates Conformity Assessment Scheme (ECAS) product scope are required to carry a UAE conformity mark (ECAS stamp and registration number). ECAS certification for cladding materials is administered through MoIAT-registered conformity assessment bodies and requires submission of product technical documentation, factory production control (FPC) audit, and in some categories, type testing at an ENAS-accredited laboratory. Specifiers should confirm ECAS registration status with suppliers for all cladding materials on projects commencing procurement after Q2 2026.

Documentation Checklist

For each cladding material proposed for DCD submission, assemble: (1) EN 13501-1 test certificate from accepted accredited laboratory, valid and product-specific; (2) Material data sheet with composition, density, and dimensions matching the tested configuration; (3) ECAS conformity certificate where required under MoIAT scope; (4) Declaration of Performance (DoP) from the manufacturer; (5) Fire engineering assessment if the material classification is below A2 or the installation detail requires engineering justification.

Section 07

DCD Technical Approval Process

All facade cladding specifications in Dubai must be submitted to DCD for Technical Approval before material installation begins on site. The Technical Approval process applies to both new construction and renovation projects affecting the external envelope. The process is administered through the DCD online portal (Montaji) for registered contractors and consultants.

Standard submission requirements:

  • Facade material schedule identifying all cladding products by manufacturer, product reference, and specification
  • EN 13501-1 fire classification certificates for each product
  • Material data sheets and product brochures
  • Facade elevation drawings indicating material zones and areas
  • Details of fixing systems, sub-frame materials, and cavity insulation
  • For ACM products: core composition certification and DCD-accepted product approval letter
  • Fire engineer’s report where required (combustible materials, complex systems, or buildings above 60 m)

Technical Approval timing:

Standard DCD Technical Approval for straightforward, fully non-combustible facade specifications typically processes within 15–30 working days. Complex submissions involving combustible elements, non-standard configurations, or novel materials may require 45–90 working days. Projects with facades above 100 metres are subject to extended review and may require an independent fire engineering peer review commissioned by DCD.

Programme Risk

DCD Technical Approval should be factored into the facade package programme from the outset. Facade material changes after DCD approval require re-submission. Where the specified material changes after initial approval — due to supply chain issues, cost substitution, or contractor-proposed alternatives — the revised material must obtain independent DCD approval before installation. Unapproved material installation is a criminal offence in Dubai and can result in mandatory removal and reinstatement at the contractor’s cost.

Section 08

Sustainability Regulations and Fire Interface

Sustainability building regulations in Dubai and Abu Dhabi introduce requirements that occasionally interact with fire compliance. The key regulatory instruments are Al Sa’fat (Dubai Green Building Regulation) and the Abu Dhabi Estidama Pearl Rating System.

Al Sa’fat (Dubai)

Al Sa’fat mandates minimum Solar Reflectance Index (SRI) values for external cladding on buildings above a threshold floor area. The SRI requirement encourages light-coloured or reflective cladding finishes to reduce urban heat island effect and building cooling loads. SRI values of 29 or above are typically required for compliance. This requirement is compatible with all standard facade cladding materials; the SRI is determined by the surface finish (colour, texture, coating) rather than the base material. However, specifiers should verify that the proposed finish colour achieves the required SRI, as darker integral pigments or coatings can reduce SRI below the threshold.

Estidama Pearl (Abu Dhabi)

Estidama Pearl 2 certification (mandatory for government-funded projects in Abu Dhabi) awards credits for facade materials with low embodied carbon, verified recycled content, and low VOC emissions. Cladding materials with EN 13501-1 Class A1 classification are generally aligned with Estidama requirements, as they are by definition inorganic and emit no combustion products. GFRC, natural stone, and solid aluminium are the most straightforward materials from a combined fire-sustainability compliance perspective.

Section 09

Specifier Compliance Checklist

The following checklist consolidates the key compliance actions for facade cladding specifications on UAE projects. It is intended as a practical reference for architects and facade consultants preparing specifications and DCD Technical Approval submissions.

At material selection stage:

  • Confirm building height above grade and identify the applicable minimum EN 13501-1 classification requirement
  • Verify that the proposed cladding material achieves this classification by independent test, not by extrapolation from similar products
  • Confirm the test certificate is product-specific, issued by an ENAS-, UKAS-, DAkkS-, or COFRAC-accredited laboratory, and within the 5-year acceptance window
  • For ACM products: verify FR mineral core classification, minimum 70% inorganic content, and DCD-accepted product approval letter
  • Confirm cavity insulation material is EN 13501-1 Class A1 or A2 (mineral wool or glass wool); exclude all EPS, XPS, and PIR from the facade cavity
  • Confirm ECAS registration status for all cladding products under MoIAT Law No. 3 of 2026 scope

At specification and tender stage:

  • Include fire classification certificate requirements explicitly in the cladding specification section
  • Specify that alternative or substituted materials must achieve equivalent EN 13501-1 classification and obtain independent DCD Technical Approval before use
  • Require contractor to submit DCD Technical Approval evidence before ordering cladding materials
  • Identify buildings requiring a fire engineer’s report: above 23 m with any combustible cladding element; above 60 m for complex facade systems; high-rise buildings with mixed-material facades

At Technical Approval submission:

  • Compile complete material schedule with product references, classifications, and certificate copies
  • Include facade elevation drawings with material zone identification
  • Submit sub-frame and fixing materials schedule confirming non-combustible fixing system
  • Allow 30–90 working days for DCD processing depending on complexity
  • Retain all approved documentation on project record for the building’s operational life
