01 / The Materials at a Glance

GFRP — Glass Fibre Reinforced Polymer

GFRP (also known as GRP — Glass Reinforced Plastic) is a composite material made from a polymer resin matrix reinforced with glass fibres. In façade applications, GFRP panels are manufactured by laminating glass fibre layers within a polyester, vinyl ester, or epoxy resin system. The result is a lightweight, high-strength panel that can be moulded into complex shapes and finished with a wide range of surface textures and colours.

In the UAE, GFRP has gained significant market share in the past decade, particularly for mid-rise residential towers, hotels, and retail developments where design flexibility and installation speed are priorities.

Aluminum — Extruded and Sheet Cladding

Aluminum cladding systems in the UAE typically take two forms: extruded aluminum profiles (used in curtain wall and rain-screen systems) and aluminium composite material (ACM) panels. Standard aluminum cladding — the focus of this comparison — refers to single-skin or cassette aluminium panels in 2–4mm thickness, typically finished with PVDF or powder coating.

Aluminum has been the dominant commercial façade material in the UAE for over three decades. Its extensive local supply chain, contractor familiarity, and competitive pricing make it the default specification for a wide range of building types.

04 / The GCC Climate Factor

The UAE's climate creates specific performance demands that are not present in European or North American markets. Both materials respond differently to these conditions:

Thermal Expansion

This is where GFRP has a significant advantage over aluminum. Aluminum has a thermal expansion coefficient of approximately 23 × 10⁻⁶ /°C — one of the highest of any common façade material. In the UAE, where façade surface temperatures can reach 80°C in summer and drop to 15°C in winter, a 3-metre aluminum panel will expand and contract by approximately 4–5mm through the annual cycle.

GFRP's thermal expansion coefficient (typically 15–20 × 10⁻⁶ /°C) is meaningfully lower, reducing movement joint requirements and stress on fixing connections. For large-format panels, this difference is significant.

GCC CLIMATE NOTE — ALUMINUM

Aluminum façade systems in the UAE must be specifically designed for the local thermal cycle. Movement joints must be generously sized — minimum 20mm at panel perimeters is recommended for Dubai projects. Contractors with European experience often undersize joints, leading to panel buckling and sealant failure within 5 years. Always require UAE-specific thermal movement calculations from your contractor.

Solar Reflectivity and Heat Gain

Aluminum's high thermal conductivity (160–200 W/mK vs GFRP's 0.3–0.5 W/mK) means it transfers heat rapidly from the outer surface to the inner face of the panel. In UAE summer conditions, this can significantly increase cooling loads for the building. GFRP is a natural thermal insulator and performs substantially better in this respect — a key consideration for Estidama Pearl Rating submissions and LEED energy modelling.

UV Degradation

Both materials can be vulnerable to UV degradation in the UAE's intense sunlight if not correctly specified. GFRP panels require a high-quality gel coat or UV-stable paint finish — cheaper gel coats will chalk and fade within 5–8 years in UAE conditions. Aluminum finished with high-quality PVDF (polyvinylidene fluoride) coating is highly resistant to UV and maintains colour integrity for 20+ years — a genuine advantage for aluminum in this climate.

ALUMINUM FIRE ADVANTAGE

Standard aluminum (single-skin, no insulated core) is classified A2 under EN 13501-1 — effectively non-combustible. This gives aluminum a significant compliance advantage over GFRP for buildings above 15 metres in the UAE. GFRP, being polymer-based, is combustible and requires DCD review for high-rise applications. For towers above 23 storeys, GFRP is generally not recommended as the primary cladding material without specialist fire engineering.

06 / Which Should You Specify?

Specify GFRP when:
  • Complex curved or moulded panel shapes required
  • Building is below 15 metres in height
  • Thermal insulation performance is a priority
  • Salt/coastal environment with high humidity
  • Budget is mid-range and design detail is key
  • Lightweight fixing structure required
  • Ornamental, retail, or hospitality project
Specify Aluminum when:
  • Building is above 15 metres — fire compliance priority
  • Long service life required — 40+ years
  • Fast programme — short lead times essential
  • Budget is tight — aluminum is lower cost
  • Commercial / office / high-rise tower
  • Sustainability target — recyclability matters
  • Flat or simple panel geometry acceptable
